Roses

Roses belong to the genus "Rosa" and are celebrated for their beauty, fragrance, and symbolism. With thousands of varieties, they come in a wide range of colors and sizes, making them a favorite in gardens and floral arrangements. Their beauty makes them a popular choice for special occasions like weddings and anniversaries.Roses have been cultivated for thousands of years, and their historical significance spans various cultures, often associated with mythology and art.
Roses thrive in full sun, requiring 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight daily for optimal blooming. Choose a location with well-draining soil rich in organic matter to prevent root rot. Regular pruning is essential for maintaining the shape and health of rose bushes. Prune in early spring to remove dead or diseased wood and to encourage new growth.

Roses are rich in symbolism, often representing love, beauty, and passion. Different colors convey different meanings; for example, red roses symbolize romantic love, while white roses represent purity and innocence.
Available Colors: Red, white, pink, yellow, orange, lavender, peach, burgundy, and green.
