Orchids

Orchids belong to the family "Orchidaceae," which is the largest family of flowering plants, boasting around 28,000 species. These stunning flowers are known for their unique shapes, vibrant colors, and intricate patterns, making them a favorite among flower enthusiasts and collectors.Many orchids are known for their delightful scents, which are often used in perfumes and scented products.Orchids have a fascinating reproductive strategy, often relying on specific insects or birds for pollination. Some species even mimic the appearance or scent of female insects to attract male pollinators!
Lilies thrive in full sun, requiring 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight daily for optimal blooming. Choose a location with well-draining soil rich in organic matter to prevent bulb rot.After blooming, remove spent flowers to encourage further growth. In colder climates, consider mulching to protect the bulbs during winter.

They are often seen as a representation of elegance and refinement, making them a popular choice for gifts and special occasions.In many traditions, orchids symbolize strength and endurance, reflecting their ability to thrive in diverse environments.
Available Colors: White, pink, yellow, orange, red, purple, cream, and peach.
