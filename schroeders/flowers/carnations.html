Carnations

Carnations, also known by their scientific name as "Dianthus Caryophyllus," are globally recognized for their vibrant colors, captivating fragrance, and symbolic significance. Cultivated for centuries, they hold a prominent place in horticulture, perfumery, and even traditional medicine.
Carnations thrive in well-drained soil and prefer plenty of sunlight. Regular watering and fertilization ensure healthy growth and abundant blooms. Deadheading, or removing spent flowers, encourages continuous flowering.

Red Carnations: Passionate love and affection.
White Carnations: Pure love, innocence, good luck, and remembrance.
Pink Carnations: Gratitude, appreciation, and remembrance, particularly for mothers.
Yellow Carnations: Joy, friendship, and rejection (in some contexts).
Available Colors: Red, white, pink, yellow, purple, green, and peach.
